About Tadoba Tiger Reserve

Tadoba Tiger Reserve, also known as Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve, is among Maharashtra's oldest and largest national parks. Established in 1955, the park was declared a Project Tiger Reserve in 1993.

Spanning over 625.40 square kilometers, this magnificent reserve is located in the Chandrapur district of Maharashtra, and derives its name from the local tribal God "Tadoba" and the Andhari River that flows through it.

History of Tadoba

The rich cultural history of Tadoba dates back centuries. According to local legends, Taru was a village chief who was killed in a mythological encounter with a tiger. A shrine dedicated to Taru still exists in the dense forest.

In 1955, an area of 116.54 sq km was declared as Tadoba National Park. Later in 1986, the adjacent Andhari Wildlife Sanctuary was integrated with it, and the combined reserve was named Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve in 1993.

The reserve became a part of Project Tiger in 1993, which was a significant step in tiger conservation efforts in India. Over the years, Tadoba has grown to become one of India's premier tiger reserves with a remarkable success story in tiger conservation.

Flora & Fauna

Flora

Tadoba's diverse ecosystem supports a rich variety of plant life. The southern tropical dry deciduous forests primarily feature teak trees, alongside bamboo, ain, bija, dhauda, hald, salai, semal, and tendu trees. These forests create a perfect habitat for the reserve's wildlife.

Fauna

Tadoba is home to approximately 88 tigers and a diverse range of wildlife. The reserve hosts numerous mammal species including leopards, sloth bears, gaur, nilgai, dhole, striped hyena, small Indian civet, jungle cats, sambar, and spotted deer. The park is also a paradise for bird watchers with over 280 species.

Safari Booking Tips

  • Book safaris at least 45-60 days in advance, especially during peak season (October to June).
  • Carry valid ID proof (Aadhar, Passport, etc.) as it's required for entry.
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing (khaki, olive, brown) to blend with the surroundings.
  • Pack essentials like sunscreen, hat, binoculars, camera, and water bottle.
  • Follow all rules and guidelines provided by your naturalist guide.

How to Reach Tadoba

By Air

The nearest airport is in Nagpur (Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport), approximately 140 km from Tadoba. From Nagpur, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Tadoba.

By Train

Chandrapur Railway Station (35 km) is the closest major railway station to Tadoba. It is well-connected to major cities like Mumbai, Nagpur, and Hyderabad. From Chandrapur, taxis and buses are available to Tadoba.

By Road

Tadoba is well-connected by road. The nearest town is Chandrapur, about 35 km away. Regular buses operate from Chandrapur, Nagpur, and other nearby cities. You can also hire a private taxi for a more comfortable journey.

Best Season to Visit

October - February

Winter Season

Winter is considered the best time to visit Tadoba. With pleasant daytime temperatures ranging from 25°C to 30°C, it's comfortable for safari activities. Wildlife is more active and visible as they gather around water sources.

25°C - 30°C High wildlife activity
March - June

Summer Season

Summer brings high temperatures (35°C to 48°C), but it's also one of the best times for tiger sightings. As water sources dry up, animals gather around the remaining water bodies, increasing chances of spotting them.

35°C - 48°C Excellent tiger sightings
July - September

Monsoon Season

The park remains closed during the monsoon season from July to September. This period allows the forest to rejuvenate and the wildlife to breed without human interference.

Heavy rainfall Park closed to visitors

Our Recommendation

For the best wildlife viewing experience, we recommend visiting Tadoba between February and May. During this period, the vegetation is less dense, water sources are limited, and wildlife concentrates around water holes, making it easier to spot tigers and other animals. Early morning safaris (6:00 AM) offer the highest chances of tiger sightings.
